Research Programs

In order to provide the contributions necessary to keep the CEGMR on its research growth track, emphasis is placed on core research programs within multi-investigator and interdisciplinary structure. The research areas are designed based on the prevalence of common diseases in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The core guiding principle that entails all the research programs is the molecular analysis of regulatory pathways and networks of potential significance at the genomic/proteomic levels involved in specific disease pathogenesis.

A comprehensive analysis of the global patterns of molecular regulation and signatures that characterize specific disease transformation at the transcriptomic, microRNA and proteomic levels with advanced computational analysis methods constitutes the core of our research programs. Each research program is formulated, managed and adequately comprehended by a team of investigators with expertise in the field that is critical to the success of the program and the CEGMR.

Our research programs aim to develop new biomarkers for early disease detection, prognosis, tumor responses, and disease recurrence. They also aim to identify mutations and molecular aberrations underlying hereditary disorders, develop biomarkers for specific stem cell types, and translate these findings into clinical and commercial applications.

Training Programs

Application of PCR in Diagnostic Molecular Genetics
Participants will learn PCR principles and applications in genetic diagnosis, including gene amplification, mutation screening, and SNP detection.
Applications of DNA Sequencing
Participants will learn advanced sequencing techniques, capillary sequencing protocols, data analysis, and data comprehension.
How to Publish in High-Impact Journals
Learn to prepare work for publication, using effective strategies and techniques for various article types and high-impact journals.
Applications of Microarray (Affymetrix & Agilent)
Applicants will understand microarrays technology, perform Affymetrix or Agilent microarrays, analyze data, and identify biomarkers.
Power of Bioinformatics
Learn to analyze and interpret large OMICs data using advanced bioinformatics software.
Applications of Clinical Cytogenetics
Acquire current knowledge and practical experience in cytogenetics.
Applications of Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization (FISH)
FISH is commonly used in clinical setting for diagnosis and studying inherited genetic diseases. Participants will learn how to get valuable information/data about the chromosomal status for any human disease, analyze the data and report them by imaging.
Principles & Applications of Cell culture
Applicants will gain practical experience and understand cell culture basics and design a cell-based research experiment.

Cancer Genomic Research Program


Saudi Arabia faces high cancer incidence, including breast, leukemia, lymphoma, thyroid, brain tumors, colorectal, and bladder cancers, according to the 2007 Cancer Registry Report.


Goals:

  • ❖Genomic research using high-throughput techniques, cell and animal models, proteomics, miRNA analysis.
  • ❖Identify biomarkers for tumor classification and stratification.
  • ❖Understand disease progression biological mechanisms for new therapies development and testing.
  • ❖Identify biomarkers for tumor classification and stratification, elucidate signaling pathways in Saudi patient samples.
  • ❖Explore potential application of research knowledge for novel therapeutic regimen design.
  • ❖Establish a collaborative research team for national projects.

Neurogenomics Research Program


The CEGMR program focuses on neuromuscular and neurodegenerative disorders in Saudi Arabia's Western region. It uses fundamental techniques and potential matrix array platforms to identify candidate gene defects in various national projects. The program aims to expand into other areas of interest.

CEGMR utilizes high-throughput techniques like SNP microarray and sequencing to identify gene defects in various disorders.

Goals

  • ❖Identify candidate genes involved in the aetiology of different neurogenetic disorders.
  • ❖Explore genetic mutations' causes of neurological diseases and their disruption.
  • ❖Promote the identification of molecular targets for the development of effective pharmacological intervention.
  • ❖Encourage developing gene expression, mutagenesis, genotyping, genomic methodologies, and linkage analysis for multigenic diseases.
  • ❖Develop a rapid, accurate molecular diagnosis for neurogenetic disorders.
  • ❖Explore genetic factors influencing behavior, cognition, sleep, learning, memory.

Genomic Screening Research Program

The Genomic Screening Research Program focuses on studying molecular genetic causes of deafness and thromophilia in Saudis, with potential for continuous expansion.

Expanding Preimplantation Genetic Diagnosis (PGD) is crucial for early prenatal diagnosis, avoiding selective termination and requiring in vitro fertilization for embryo evaluation, complementing assisted reproductive technology.


Goals

  • ❖Identifying genetic diseases' molecular causes in Saudi Arabia and developing biomarkers for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment.
  • ❖Introduce state-of-the art technology such as pyrosequencing, dHPLC, microarrays for mutation and subsequent gene expression analysis.
  • ❖Provide PGD services for screening genetic abnormalities.
  • ❖Explore PGD applications in complex traits like diabetes, CHD, and cancers.
  • ❖Possibly augment PGD and stem cell therapy as a therapeutic intervention.

Tissue Culture & Regenerative Medicine Research Program


Stem cells are plastic cells that develop into various types during early development and help maintain adult tissue by self-renewal and generating differentiated progeny.

Stem cells have potential for regenerative medicine, but extensive research and multidisciplinary efforts are needed to make them a reality. The CEGMR focuses on common genetic diseases, hematopoietic stem cells, and leukemia stem cell research.


Goals

  • ❖A robust stem cell research platform with advanced facilities, including high-throughput analysis, animal testing, and irradiation units.
  • ❖Generate, characterize iPSC from healthy donors, differentiate to treat specific disorders.
  • ❖Utilize animal transplantation models (NOD/SCID or other mice strains) to address safety concerns in iPSC development.
  • ❖Produce hematopoietic stem cells and mesenchymal stem cells from bone marrow, peripheral blood, and umbilical cord blood.
  • ❖conduct research into new sources of HSC and mesenchymal stem cells such as placenta, iPSC etc,.
  • ❖Research expanding UCB-derived HSC for single donor transplantation in leukemia and disorders.
  • ❖The establishment of a cGMP facility to scale up the expansion of transplantable stem cells or their product.

Pharmacogenomics Research Program


Cancer chemotherapy faces challenges due to individual therapeutic responses and toxicity, while widespread antimicrobial resistance limits treatment options for multidrug resistant infections. CEGMR started a major research initiative in the area of pharmacogenomics and drug discovery that focuses on pharmacogenomics and drug discovery to address unmet medical needs in cancer and infectious diseases.


Goals

  • ❖Establishment of a research platform to examine genetic variations' impact on Saudi Arabian anticancer drug response.
  • ❖Develop personalized patient risk profiles and treatment strategies for diseases.
  • ❖Establishment of a comprehensive cancer research platform using natural, synthetic, biotechnological tools.
  • ❖Initiating an advanced facility for antimicrobial compound discovery targeting multidrug resistant pathogens.

Biorepositories & Biospecimen Research Program


The Human Genome Project (HGP) in 2003 led to a surge in biobanks, focusing on personalized diagnostics and therapeutics. The Biobanking & Biospecimen Research Program (BBRP) in Saudi Arabia aims to provide a premium biorepository of human specimens and annotated data, fostering state-of-the-art research and bridging the gap between clinicians and scientists.

BBRP focuses on biomedical research for innovative diagnostic, prognostic, and therapeutic approaches in Saudi medical conditions.

In order to move towards personalized medicine using the latest OMICs tools, the BBRP strives for national collaborations with all healthcare provider partners to collect, process, store and release high quality biospecimens with full annotation data according to the latest SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) in compliance with the national and international norms and rules.

BBRP promotes biobanking and biospecimens donation among healthcare providers, policymakers, patients, and the general population for research and validation cohorts.

BBRP aims to enhance biospecimens management for efficient diagnostics and therapeutics, benefiting millions of patients worldwide.


Goals

  • ❖Support Saudi healthcare system for research, prevention, and treatment of common diseases.
  • ❖Implementation of state-of-the-art biobanking program by using the best international practice guidelines.
  • ❖ Design and implement customized educational, training and outreach programs in order to increase awareness and consciousness about modern biobanking in the post-genomic era.
  • ❖Contribute to the maturation of plans and standards for a national biobanking network.

Tissue Culture & Regenerative Medicine Research Program


Research challenges involve understanding how a single cell, like the fertilized egg, gives rise to a fully formed organism. This process involves dividing various cell types and subtypes within a complex architecture. Cell signaling controls this orchestrated process, which is critical in human disease. Understanding these mechanisms is essential for improving disease processes and gene expression.

Our research in the Functional and Developmental Genomics program utilizes genomic, genetic, molecular, and cell biological approaches to understand developmental mechanisms, using bio-imaging, large-scale genomics, and cross-species comparisons.


Goals

  • ❖Understanding cell transformation into fully functional organism through early and late embryonic development.
  • ❖Unraveling the molecular mechanisms involved in reproduction, embryonic development and organogenesis.
  • ❖Utilize CEGMR's advanced technologies for understanding embryonic cell signaling.
  • ❖Explore causes of abnormal development using approaches such as gene editing and gain-/loss technologies.
  • ❖Explore gene-environment interactions' impact on embryonic development and their connection to congenital malformations and diseases.
